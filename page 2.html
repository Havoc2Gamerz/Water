Check Faucets and Pipes for Leaks

Checking for leaks will have a large impact on water conservation. Small drops of water can waste about 20 gallons of water per day. Larger leaks can be more up to hundreds of gallons. Checking for leaks will conserve water and will save you a lot of money on the water bill.

Turning off the water when not using it

When you're brushing teeth or shaving or washing your car make sure to turn off the water. Doing that will conserve lots of water. Turning off the water when you're not using it, when you shower and brush your teeth can save up 8 gallons of water a day that would be more 200 gallons of water a month.

Bathing

When you shower you can use up to 20 to 40 gallons of water. Turning off the water when soaping up can save a lot of water. Also taking more baths can save more water than showering everyday.

Tiolet

Flushing material that is not supposed to be flush will cause problems for your plumbing, sewer system and environment. The material will have a hard time dissolving or at all and will cause issues with your toilet. If a material that doesn't dissolve well gets caught and nick or bends in a pipe it can create a block in the pipe and cause a sewer backup in a home or neighborhood.

Collecting Water

Collecting rainwater can have lots of benefits. Collecting rainwater will conserve water because instead of having to use water from your home you can use rainwater. You can use rainwater for your garden, compost, and washing vehicles. Because you are using rainwater you will save money on the water bill.
